Night Vision Mode
Tints the whole app dim red to protect your dark-adapted eyes at the telescope. Stays on as you move between pages.
Sky Finder Zoom
Camera field of view
If the Moon or Milky Way markers drift as you pan the camera — sitting right when centered but sliding off toward the edges — nudge this until a marker tracks a known object across the whole frame. Lower = the markers move faster across the screen.
Narrow (15°) 70° Wide (110°)
Tip: first use Align in the Sky Finder to fix the starting point, then fine-tune this zoom so a marker stays locked on a known object as you sweep across the whole frame.
Sky Finder Alignment
Quick Align (recommended)
If the star and planet markers sit in the wrong place, the fix takes one tap. Open the Sky Finder, tap Align in the tools row, and the app picks the brightest unmistakable thing in the sky right now — the Moon, a bright planet, or a bright star — and tells you exactly what to aim at. Center it in the crosshair and tap Align. That\u2019s it. Phone compasses are often off by 10\u201330\u00B0; this corrects it in a second.
No need to know any stars by name — the app names the target for you and points you to it. Daytime works too: if nothing else is up it aligns to the Sun (aim using the screen only; don\u2019t look at the Sun directly).
Manual nudge
If you\u2019d rather fine-tune by hand, or your compass is only slightly off, nudge the whole sky left/right (compass) and up/down (altitude) until the markers sit on the real objects. These add to whatever Quick Align set.
Left / Right (compass) 0\u00B0
Up / Down (altitude) 0\u00B0
Reset clears both Quick Align and the manual nudge, returning the Sky Finder to the raw compass reading.
